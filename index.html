Abstract

Prostate multiparametric MRI (mpMRI) has transformed cancer diagnosis by enabling image-guided risk assessment, yet its interpretation remains challenging because it requires coordinated anatomical localization, lesion delineation and diagnostic classification across heterogeneous imaging sequences. Current medical AI systems are often optimized for isolated tasks and therefore poorly aligned with the sequential reasoning used in clinical prostate MRI assessment. Here we introduce ProGen, an anatomy-guided generalist foundation model for prostate MRI. ProGen is pretrained on Pro-7M, a large-scale curated corpus comprising 39,587 MRI volumes and over 7 million slices aggregated from 27 retrospective public prostate MRI datasets across five mpMRI modalities, including T2W, T1W, ADC, DWI and DCE. Because Pro-7M is constructed as a broad pretraining corpus, some downstream benchmark cohorts are represented at the source-dataset level during pretraining; downstream experiments are therefore framed as task-specific held-out evaluations rather than dataset-external validation. Using a modality-aware 3D masked autoencoding framework with boundary-preserving and asymmetry-aware anatomical priors, ProGen is adapted to eight spatial and diagnostic tasks spanning prostate anatomy segmentation, lesion delineation, intraprostatic target identification, clinically significant prostate cancer diagnosis and biopsy-decision support. ProGen achieves a whole-gland segmentation Dice similarity coefficient of 91.8%, improves clinically significant prostate cancer lesion delineation Dice by 17.7 percentage points over nnU-Net, and reaches AUROCs of 0.890 for patient-level clinically significant prostate cancer diagnosis and 0.817 for PSA 4–10 ng/ml gray-zone triage. These results show that anatomy-guided foundation modeling can unify spatial and diagnostic reasoning in prostate MRI and support safer, more quantitative pathways for non-invasive prostate cancer management.
